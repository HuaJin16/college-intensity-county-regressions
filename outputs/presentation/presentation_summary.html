County College Intensity - Presentation Summary

Cross-sectional county regressions for rent and wage outcomes (50 states + DC + Puerto Rico).

Generated: 2026-04-18 19:28

Executive Takeaways

  • Rent baseline: college intensity is positively associated with rent (beta=0.0015, p<0.001), about 0.15% higher rent per +1 pp college intensity.
  • Wage baseline: association with wage is near zero and statistically weak (beta=0.0001, p=0.685, ~0.01% per +1 pp).
  • Rent two-part: the college-presence indicator is near zero once college size is separated out, while the conditional intensity term remains positive and precise.
  • Wage two-part: neither the presence indicator nor the pooled conditional-intensity term is precise in the full-sample decomposition.
  • Interpretation: estimates are associational (cross-sectional), not causal.
